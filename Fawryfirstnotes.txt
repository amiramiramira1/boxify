
## 1. Business Overview

### Business History
Fawry for Banking Technology and Electronic Payments was **founded in 2008** as a pioneer in the Egyptian e-payment space. The company's core mission was to address the challenge of bill payments and financial transactions for the entire Egyptian population, including the **unbanked** segment. A significant corporate milestone was its **Initial Public Offering (IPO)** on the Egyptian Exchange (EGX: FWRY.CA) in **2019**. Since then, Fawry has transitioned from a pure bill payment service provider to Egypt's leading provider of diversified e-payments and digital finance solutions.

### Business Model
Fawry operates as a **multi-channel payment platform** that acts as an intermediary, connecting customers, businesses (corporates and SMEs), and banks to a comprehensive suite of digital services.

* **P2P Model (Acceptance)**: Through its peer-to-peer (P2P) model, Fawry enables corporations and SMEs to accept electronic payments via various platforms. This involves integrating with service providers and allowing consumers to pay at multiple points.
* **Revenue Diversification**: The business model focuses on diversifying revenue streams beyond its traditional core of **Alternative Digital Payments (ADP)** (bill payments) into higher-margin, high-growth segments. Key segments include:
    * **Banking Services/Acceptance** (Accepting card and digital payments via POS and online channels).
    * **Financial Services** (Microfinance and digital credit).
    * **Supply Chain Solutions** (Digitalizing payments and workflows for the supply chain).
* **Extensive Network**: Fawry’s operational advantage lies in its expansive physical and digital network:
    * Over **400,000 agents/merchants** (POS terminals) as of 1H2025.
    * A network of **36 contracted banks**.
    * Digital channels including its **mobile application** and **websites**.

### Expansion Strategy
Fawry’s strategy is built on innovation, diversification, and market leadership in the Egyptian fintech landscape.

1.  **Vertical Integration and Diversification**: The main focus is on accelerating the growth of its non-core segments, particularly **Banking Services (Acceptance)**, which saw significant revenue increases (e.g., 116.4% y-o-y in 4Q2024). This diversification shields the company from reliance on traditional services and captures more of the value chain.
2.  **Digital Finance Leadership**: Expansion into digital lending through **Fawry Microfinance (Fawry MSME)** is a major strategic pillar, aiming to support small and micro-entrepreneurs and increase financial inclusion.
3.  **Efficiency and Technology**: Maintaining **strong cost discipline** and investing in proprietary technology are central to achieving enhanced operational efficiency and high margins (e.g., EBITDA margin reaching 49.9% in FY2024).
4.  **Strategic Partnerships**: The company continuously forms new collaborations to expand its service offerings and reach, such as the partnership with Ottu to drive unprecedented business growth.

## 2. ESG (Environmental, Social, and Governance)

While the earnings releases primarily focus on financial performance, supplementary information highlights Fawry’s commitment to ESG principles.

* **Social (S) - Financial Inclusion**: The company's business model is inherently a social driver, as it is the largest platform serving both the banked and **unbanked** populations in Egypt, promoting widespread **financial inclusion**. The Microfinance arm, **Fawry MSME**, explicitly supports over 120,000 Egyptian entrepreneurs, contributing to economic growth and employment rates in local communities.
* **Environmental (E) - Digitalization**: Fawry contributes to a more sustainable and environmentally conscious economy by enabling **cashless collections** and **digital transformation** for businesses. Its core service, enabling electronic payments, directly reduces the need for physical cash transactions, contributing to operational efficiencies and less paper usage.
* **Governance (G)**: Fawry is committed to **good governance systems**, emphasizing the separation of ownership and management. It ensures that the board of directors includes experienced members and utilizes oversight committees to maintain accountability and alignment with stakeholder interests.

## 3. Industry Overview and Analysis

Fawry operates at the heart of the Egyptian **e-payments and digital finance industry**, which is characterized by rapid growth and a governmental push for digital transformation.

* **Market Leadership**: Fawry maintains its position as **Egypt's leading provider** in this space.
* **Rapid Digital Adoption**: The industry exhibits strong underlying growth, reflected in Fawry’s **Throughput Value**, which consistently increased year-over-year (e.g., 69.5% in FY2023 and 72.9% in FY2024).
* **Key Growth Drivers**: The industry's expansion is fueled by:
    * **Governmental Mandates**: Official support for cashless payments and financial inclusion initiatives.
    * **Demographics**: A large, young, and digitally-savvy population driving the adoption of mobile and online payments.
    * **Ecosystem Integration**: The ability of Fawry and other players to seamlessly integrate with government services, banks, and merchant systems to create a unified payment ecosystem.
* **Competitive Dynamics**: While the analysis doesn't detail direct competitors, Fawry's strategy of vertical diversification into Acceptance and Digital Finance suggests it is actively competing in new financial service verticals beyond its traditional utility payment space.

## 4. Products and Services Overview

Fawry's extensive product portfolio is divided into several synergistic business segments:

| Segment | Core Services | Key Features and Expansion |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Alternative Digital Payments (ADP)** | Electronic bill payments, utility bills, mobile top-ups, provisions, e-ticketing, cable TV, and other digital services. | This is the traditional, foundational business segment, providing recurring revenue from millions of users. |
| **Banking Services (Acceptance)** | Enabling corporates and SMEs to accept electronic payments via Point-of-Sale (POS) devices, e-commerce websites, and mobile applications. | A high-growth area driving significant revenue increase, as Fawry acts as a major Merchant Acquirer in Egypt. |
| **Financial Services** | **Fawry Microfinance (Fawry MSME)** provides loans to micro and small entrepreneurs. Offers other credit and consumer finance products (like BNPL). | The segment focuses on digital lending, with its Total Gross Loan Portfolio expanding rapidly (e.g., 121.0% y-o-y in 1H2025). |
| **Supply Chain Solutions** | Services focused on optimizing payments, financing, and transactions within B2B supply chains. | This segment represents a strategic move into B2B digital transformation. |
| **FawryPay** (Digital Gateway) | Fawry’s digital payment gateway enabling online and in-app transactions. | Used by partners like Ottu, it ensures flexible, secure, and seamless payment processing. |
| **FawryPlus** | A network of branches or service centers that offer a wider range of financial and banking services. | Used for loan disbursement and other specialized services. |
